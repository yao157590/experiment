Pan-Seared Scallops on Cauliflower Purée

Introduction & Flavor Profile

This dish is a classic culinary masterpiece that balances delicate textures and sophisticated flavors. The star of the plate features sea scallops pan-seared to perfection, creating a deeply caramelized, golden-brown crust while maintaining a sweet, tender, and succulent center.

The scallops are nested on a bed of velvety cauliflower purée. The purée offers a smooth, creamy contrast with subtle nutty undertones from the blended cauliflower, butter, and cream. To elevate the dish, a sharp, vibrant caper-raisin dressing is drizzled over the top, introducing a beautiful harmony of sweet, briny, and acidic notes that cut through the richness of the purée.

Ingredients

For the Scallops:

  • 6 large fresh sea scallops (tough side muscles removed)
  • 1 tablespoon neutral high-smoke-point oil (such as grapeseed or vegetable oil)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • A pinch of flaky sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper

For the Cauliflower Purée:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower (cut into small florets)
  • 2 cups whole milk (or vegetable stock)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • Salt and white pepper to taste

For the Caper-Raisin Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons capers (drained and rinsed)
  • 2 tablespoons golden raisins (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon flat-leaf parsley (finely chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice

Cooking Procedure

  1. Prepare the Cauliflower Purée: Place the cauliflower florets into a medium saucepan and pour in the whole milk until the cauliflower is mostly submerged. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Cook for 12 to 15 minutes, or until the florets are completely fork-tender. Drain the cauliflower, reserving a splash of the warm cooking milk. Transfer the cooked cauliflower into a high-powered blender. Add the butter, heavy cream, a pinch of salt, and a pinch of white pepper. Blend on high until completely smooth and velvety. If the mixture is too thick, add a tablespoon or two of the reserved milk. Taste and adjust seasoning, then cover to keep warm.
  2. Mix the Dressing: In a small mixing bowl, combine the drained capers, chopped golden raisins, and finely chopped parsley. Whisk in the extra virgin olive oil and fresh lemon juice until well incorporated. Set aside at room temperature to let the flavors marry.
  3. Sear the Scallops: Thoroughly pat the scallops dry with paper towels. Moisture is the enemy of a good sear; they must be completely dry to form a crust. Season both sides of the scallops generously with sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper just before cooking. Heat a heavy-bottomed skillet or cast-iron pan over high heat until it begins to smoke slightly. Pour in the neutral oil. Carefully place the scallops in the pan one by one, leaving plenty of space between them. Press down gently on each scallop for the first few seconds to ensure even contact with the hot surface. Sear undisturbed for 90 seconds to 2 minutes until a deep, golden-brown crust forms on the bottom. Flip the scallops over. Add the tablespoon of butter to the pan. As it melts, tilt the pan slightly and spoon the foaming butter over the tops of the scallops for another 30 to 45 seconds. Remove the scallops from the pan immediately to prevent overcooking. They should feel slightly springy to the touch.
  4. Assemble the Plate: Spoon a generous pool of the warm cauliflower purée onto the center of a shallow plate or bowl. Use the back of the spoon to smooth it out into an elegant circle. Arrange three pan-seared scallops gracefully on top of the purée, keeping the golden crust facing upward. Spoon the caper-raisin dressing over and around the scallops, ensuring a bit of the briny, sweet mixture accentuates each bite. Serve immediately.
